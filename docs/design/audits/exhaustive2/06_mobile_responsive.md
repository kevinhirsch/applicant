# Applicant Front-Door — Mobile & Responsive Audit (per surface, 320/375/768/834)

**Lens:** the realistic mobile story — a push arrives, the user opens the Portal on a phone,
approves a digest row, reviews a redline, maybe authorizes a final submit. Every applicant
surface audited by reading the CSS + JS (media queries, fixed widths, dvh, inline `--window-w`,
touch/gesture handlers, PWA plumbing) at 320/375/768/834 widths, portrait + landscape.

**Dedup:** goes deeper than `PRODUCT_EXHAUSTIVE_AUDIT.md` and `exhaustive/ux-flows-ia.md`
Tier 5 (#31–40). Items there (remote iframe "cramped" #33, inline `--window-w` split #34,
hidden close-X risk #35, stacking #36, dock registration #37) are **not repeated** — where a
mobile finding extends one, the extension is new and finer.

**Context:** on ≤768px every `.modal-content` becomes a full-width bottom sheet
(`workspace/static/style.css:4609-4690`) — 85vh cap, grab handle, swipe-down dismiss
(`workspace/static/js/ui.js:950-1135`), close X hidden `!important` (`style.css:4687-4689`).
All anchors below are under `workspace/` unless prefixed `src/` or `frontend/`.

Format: `N. **Title** — [VALUE · EFFORT] — rationale + anchor.`

## Tier 1 — broken or materially wrong on a phone (fix first)

1. **Redline diff collapses into a single unreadable blob in the front-door** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — The engine emits the redline as per-line inline `<span>/<del>/<ins>` joined with `"\n"` (`src/applicant/adapters/resume_tailoring/latex_tailor.py:229`) and its own built-in UI renders it with `#redline-view { white-space: pre-wrap; font-family: ui-monospace }` (`frontend/static/applicant/applicant.css:69`). The workspace review panel injects the same `rendered_html` verbatim (`static/js/documentLibrary.js:2277-2281`) into a box with **no white-space rule** (`:2271-2273`), so every newline collapses to a space and the entire resume diff renders as one run-on paragraph. On a 320px phone inside the 200px-tall box this is the product's core review moment, broken. Add `white-space:pre-wrap` (+ monospace) to `.doclib-applicant-redline`.

2. **Redline add/strike colors don't exist in the front-door stylesheet** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — `.redline-add` / `.redline-sub` / `.redline-eq` have **zero rules** in `static/style.css` (grep: 0 matches); only the engine's internal UI styles them (`frontend/static/applicant/applicant.css:66-68`). The phone reviewer sees UA-default `<ins>` underline / `<del>` strikethrough with no green/red backgrounds — additions and subtractions are nearly indistinguishable at 12px on a small screen. Port the two rules (theme-aware, light/dark).

3. **Swipe-down dismisses the blocking onboarding wizard** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — The sheet swipe-dismiss handler applies to *every* `.modal-content` and simply adds `.hidden` to the parent modal (`static/js/ui.js:1119-1127`). The one deliberately blocking, non-dismissible overlay (`#applicant-onboarding-overlay`, backdrop click swallowed at `static/js/applicantOnboarding.js:175`) never sets the `[data-no-swipe-dismiss]` opt-out (`ui.js:993`; only research/signature/document use it). A stray downward drag from the top-48px zone mid-intake dismisses required setup with a half-filled form. Add the opt-out attribute (or an id guard in ui.js).

4. **Android back button exits the app instead of closing the sheet** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — No applicant surface pushes a history entry (no `pushState`/`hashchange` anywhere in the applicant JS — confirmed by ux-flows #1 for deep-linking; the *mobile* consequence is new): on a phone, the most instinctive dismiss gesture — system back — navigates away from the whole app (or out past login) while the Portal/redline sheet is open. Push a `history.state` per open sheet and close-on-popstate; this is independent of (and cheaper than) full hash routing.

5. **The 85dvh sheet token is dead code — declaration order is inverted** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `--window-sheet-max-h: 85dvh` (`static/style.css:96`) is written **before** its own fallback: `max-height:var(--window-sheet-max-h); max-height:85vh;` (`style.css:4618-4620`, repeated with `!important` at `:4679-4681`). Both declarations are valid, so the later `85vh` always wins and the sheet sizes against the *large* viewport — on Firefox Android / iOS Safari the sheet is taller than the intended 85dvh whenever the URL bar is shown, exactly what the adjacent comment says dvh was added to prevent (`:4529-4534`). Swap the two lines in both places.

6. **Portal browser notifications silently never fire on Android** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — `_maybeDesktopNotify` uses the page constructor `new Notification(...)` (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:158-166`). Android Chrome throws "Illegal constructor" for page-created notifications when a service worker is registered (which it is: `static/index.html:2623`) — notifications must go through `registration.showNotification()`. The `try/catch` swallows the throw, so the action-needed ping no-ops on precisely the platform the mobile story targets. Route through the SW registration when available.

7. **No web-push channel for the action-needed moments** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: L] — The SW has no `push`/`notificationclick` handlers (`static/sw.js:1-143`) and there is no `pushManager`/VAPID anywhere in `workspace/` (grep: 0). Phone alerts depend entirely on the user configuring ntfy/Discord/email fan-out (`static/js/applicantOnboarding.js:441-545`). An installed PWA with a registered SW is one VAPID keypair away from first-party "review needed" pushes that deep-link into the Portal — the single highest-leverage mobile capability the product lacks.

8. **Portal/redline/digest have zero dedicated CSS — inline styles mean no mobile overrides are possible** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — `applicant-digest-*` and `doclib-applicant-*` classes have **no rules at all** in `static/style.css` (grep: 0), and Portal rows are styled inline (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:638-648`). Every sizing decision (12px fonts, 200px boxes, 24px buttons) is hard-coded in JS strings, so a `@media (max-width:768px)` fix *cannot be written* without first moving these to classes. This is the structural blocker in front of most Tier-2 items below — do it once.

## Tier 2 — the daily loop on a phone (Portal → digest → redline → approve)

9. **"N need you" is invisible on mobile — the rail (and its badge) is hidden** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — On ≤768px the icon rail is `display:none !important` (`static/style.css:3832`), shown only as a hamburger-summoned overlay (`.mobile-mini`, `static/js/sidebar-layout.js:43,447-448`). The Portal's red count badge (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:1244-1258`) therefore polls and updates a hidden element; a phone user gets no ambient pending-count anywhere. Surface the count on something always visible on mobile — the status strip, the hamburger button, or an app-badge via `navigator.setAppBadge()` for the installed PWA.

10. **The most-tapped mobile controls are 24px tall** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — Digest row actions Approve / Pass / Research / Open are `.memory-toolbar-btn` (`static/js/emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js:290-329`) with fixed `height:24px; font-size:11px` (`static/style.css:7399-7404`); the digest header's four toolbar buttons reuse it (`applicantDigest.js:168-181`). The codebase's own touch standard is 44px (`.cal-btn min-height:44px`, `style.css:30786`; the pause pill grows its hit area to ≥44px, `:35917-35921`). Approving a job on a phone — the core daily action — has the smallest target in the product. Add a ≤768px min-height/hit-area rule.

11. **Redline confined to a fixed 200px window regardless of viewport** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — `max-height:200px; overflow:auto` inline (`static/js/documentLibrary.js:2273`). On a phone the full resume diff must be reviewed through a ~5-line slot inside an 85vh sheet that has spare room below. Size it with dvh (e.g. `max-height:45dvh`) or `flex:1` within the sheet body.

12. **Sub-16px inputs trigger iOS focus-zoom across the loop** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — iOS Safari auto-zooms any focused input under 16px: the Portal answer textarea is 12px (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:654-658`), missing-info inputs 12px (`:679-682`), the applicant chat composer 13px (`static/js/applicantChat.js:180-181`), the styled-prompt input 0.9rem≈14.4px on mobile (`static/style.css:4930-4932`). The zoom leaves the sheet horizontally cropped after blur. Set 16px on ≤768px (the viewport meta rightly does *not* disable user scaling — `static/index.html:234` — so fix the font size, not the zoom).

13. **No iOS keyboard avoidance for bottom-sheet inputs** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — Keyboard handling rests on `interactive-widget=resizes-content` (`static/index.html:234`), which iOS ignores. The main chat has `visualViewport` compensation (`static/js/chat.js:3290-3294`, `app.js:1916-1927`), but no applicant modal does: the sticky composer (`static/js/applicantChat.js:179-183`), Portal answer/missing-info fields (`applicantPortal.js:654-682`), the redline request-change textarea (`static/js/documentLibrary.js:2324-2327`) and the mandatory pass-reason prompt all live in a flex-end-anchored sheet the iOS keyboard slides *over*. Add a shared visualViewport offset for the sheet (translateY by `window.innerHeight - vv.height`).

14. **No swipe-to-approve/decline on Portal or digest rows (opportunity)** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: L] — Zero touch handlers exist in `applicantPortal.js` (click-only wiring `: 951-1214`) or `applicantDigest.js`; approving each of N digest rows is two precise small-button taps. The house already has a horizontal-swipe engine for toasts (`static/js/ui.js:241-292`, 70px threshold) — a row-level swipe-right = approve / swipe-left = snooze-or-pass with an undo toast would make phone triage feel native. Must coexist with the document-level tab-swipe (see #38) and sheet `touch-action:pan-y` (`style.css:4635`).

15. **Final authorize is one thumb-tap on mobile** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — The irreversible submit confirm is a single styled-confirm button (`static/js/applicantRemote.js:503-519`, `_confirm` fallback `window.confirm` at `:427-432`; Portal path `static/js/applicantPortal.js:77-82` used at `:1154`). On touch, the confirm button lands near where the triggering thumb already rests — accidental double-tap is a real failure mode for the one action the product promises never to do without the user. On ≤768px, weight it: press-and-hold, a slide-to-confirm, or a 2-step sheet. (Extends the desktop-focused trust findings; the mobile tap mechanics are the new part.)

16. **Mandatory 280-char typed reason to pass a role** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — Digest "Pass" requires a typed reason via `styledPrompt` (`static/js/emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js:373-387`), and on mobile the styled-prompt deliberately keeps the bottom-anchor (`static/style.css:4541-4546`) — under the iOS keyboard gap (#13). Typing prose per rejected role on a phone kills triage; offer tap-chips ("salary too low", "wrong location", "not my stack", "other…") that map to the same feedback signal.

17. **Portal sentence-length buttons: `nowrap` with no ellipsis at 320px** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `#applicant-portal-modal .cal-btn … { white-space:nowrap; max-width:100% }` (`static/style.css:32348-32354`) keeps labels like "I'll submit it myself (open live session)" (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:779-786`) on one line; at 320px (sheet content ≈290px) a label longer than the row clips silently — `max-width` bounds the box but nothing adds `overflow:hidden`/`text-overflow`. Add ellipsis, or allow 2-line wrap with `min-height` intact on ≤768px.

18. **Hover-only `title` tooltips have no touch equivalent — full inventory** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — No long-press or tap-popover alternative exists anywhere in the applicant JS (no `contextmenu`/long-press handlers). Meaning delivered *only* via `title=`: Portal Snooze/Done (`applicantPortal.js:633,636`), "What it never does" header button (`:322-324`), momentum chips (`:521,532`), 2FA retry (`:759`), authorize buttons (`:782-785`), Vault save hints (`static/js/applicantVault.js:77,111`), status strip (`static/index.html:1202`). The wizard solved this correctly — its `.ao-tip` is `tabindex="0"` with a `:focus::after` bubble (`static/js/applicantOnboarding.js:221`, `static/style.css:32269-32278`); adopt that pattern (or visible microcopy) for the loop surfaces.

19. **Session hand-off codes rely on `user-select:all` — no Copy button** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — The emergency hand-off renders pasteable values as `<code style="user-select:all;word-break:break-all">` (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:697-710`). On iOS Safari `user-select:all` support is inconsistent; long-press selects a word, not the token — copying a session URL/credential on the phone (the exact device the hand-off targets) is fiddly. Add a `navigator.clipboard.writeText` copy button per value.

20. **"Approve all N" is an 11px/2px-padding micro-button for a bulk action** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — The bulk digest approval renders inline `font-size:11px;padding:2px 8px` (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:867-868`) — the highest-consequence tap on the Portal is also below target size on touch (compare #10). Give it ≥44px hit area and a count-confirm on ≤768px.

## Tier 3 — remote live-view & takeover on touch

21. **Live view: no orientation story at all** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — The iframe is a fixed `height:40dvh` strip (`static/js/applicantRemote.js:74-79`) and the file registers zero `resize`/`orientationchange`/`ResizeObserver` handlers. Rotating to landscape (the natural move to read a desktop Workday page) yields a ~130px-tall view inside a sheet that is itself 85vh of a ~360px-tall viewport. Detect landscape and go full-bleed (100dvh, chrome collapsed), or offer a Fullscreen-API button on the frame (manifest `orientation:"any"` — `static/manifest.json:8` — permits it).

22. **No zoom affordance into the live page** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — The remote page renders desktop-sized inside the 40dvh frame; the surrounding sheet locks gestures to `touch-action:pan-y` (`static/style.css:4635`) and the module offers no zoom/pan controls (`static/js/applicantRemote.js:74-90`). Whether pinch works depends entirely on the embedded viewer document. Add explicit zoom in/out/fit controls on the frame chrome, or CSS-scale the iframe with a pan wrapper.

23. **"Take control" on a phone is watch-only with no guidance** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — Takeover is a server-side handoff (`POST …/takeover`, `static/js/applicantRemote.js:367-379`) with **no** pointer/touch forwarding — the user must then operate the session through the embedded view or "Open in new tab" (`:89-90, 381-386`). On mobile nothing explains this; the honest UX is a ≤768px hint ("Best on a larger screen — or open in a new tab and rotate") plus making open-in-new-tab the primary CTA on touch.

24. **2FA / sign-in Portal rows funnel into the cramped Remote sheet** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — "Continue Google sign-in" / "Try Google again" (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:757-759`) deep-link into the Remote surface, i.e. the 40dvh strip (#21), to perform the touch-hostile task (typing a 2FA code into a remote desktop browser). For these rows specifically, open the session `view_url` in a new tab on ≤768px instead of the embedded frame.

25. **Remote session picker capped at 46% width** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `max-width:46%` inline (`static/js/applicantRemote.js:63-64`): at 320px that is ~135px for "Company — Role" labels; no mobile override widens it. Let it take 100% width in its own row on ≤768px.

## Tier 4 — gestures, dismissal, and scroll conflicts

26. **Mid-scroll surfaces have no visible dismiss on mobile** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — Concrete extension of ux-flows #35: the swipe-dismiss only arms from the header/top-48px or when the body is at `scrollTop === 0` (`static/js/ui.js:995-1006`), and the close X is hidden `!important` (`static/style.css:4687-4689`). Portal, Activity, Chat and Vault all have `overflow-y:auto` bodies (`applicantPortal.js:327`, `applicantActivity.js:251`, `applicantChat.js:83`, `applicantVault.js:53`) — deep in the pending list, dismissal needs scroll-to-top-then-swipe or a precise grab-handle hit, with nothing telling the user that. Keep a mobile X (or a "Done" footer) on scrollable applicant sheets.

27. **Pull-to-refresh is disabled with no in-sheet substitute — and the gesture slot is taken by dismiss** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — `overscroll-behavior:none` on `html,body` (`static/style.css:151`) kills browser PTR, and inside the sheet a downward pull at `scrollTop 0` *is* the dismiss gesture (`static/js/ui.js:1006`). So the natural "pull to refresh my pending list" motion closes the Portal instead; refresh is a 24px header button (`applicantPortal.js:322`). Either wire a short-pull = refresh / long-pull = dismiss split on the sheet body, or visibly refresh on `visibilitychange` re-entry (the poll infra `pollVisible` already exists, `applicantPortal.js:1508`).

28. **Document-level horizontal tab-swipe can hijack scrolls near applicant scrollers** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — The global mobile tab-swiper listens on `document` and fires on any ≥60px mostly-horizontal swipe when a `.admin-tabs`/`.memory-tabs` bar is visible (`static/app.js:2909-2955`). The redline box scrolls horizontally (`overflow:auto`, `documentLibrary.js:2273` — long LaTeX lines) and digest rows sit inside modals with tab bars; a horizontal diff-scroll that ends past 60px can click an unrelated tab. Add applicant scroll containers to the `_IGNORE` selector (`app.js:2917-2918`).

29. **Sheet-swipe vs. inner `<select>`s and sliders in the wizard** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — The drag starts from any touch in the top 48px or at scroll-top (`ui.js:995-1006`); the wizard's first visible rows are often selects/inputs near the top of a freshly-opened (scrollTop 0) step. The `_IGNORE`-style guard that the tab-swiper has (`app.js:2917`) does not exist in the sheet-dismiss path — it only exempts scrollable ancestors and `[data-no-swipe-dismiss]` (`ui.js:993,1035-1052`). Exempt form controls (`input,textarea,select`) from initiating sheet drags.

30. **No pressed-state feedback on the primary buttons for touch** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `.cal-btn` defines `:hover` only (`static/style.css:30786-30787`); there is no `:active` rule, and the applicant surfaces add none. On a phone (no hover) taps on Approve/Snooze/Done give zero visual acknowledgment until the network round-trip completes. Add a shared `:active` transform/opacity for `.cal-btn`/`.memory-toolbar-btn` under `@media (hover:none)` (the codebase already uses that pattern for gallery/code buttons, `style.css:3479,13921`).

## Tier 5 — the narrow top: status strip, pause, safe areas, toasts

31. **Status strip + pause pill + centered meta overlay collide at 320–375px** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — On mobile `.chat-meta-overlay` becomes `position:absolute`, centered, `max-width:55%` (`static/style.css:3822-3829` block) *in the same top bar* as the inline status strip (`max-width:46vw`, `:32159`) and the ≥44px pause pill (`:35896-35904`). 46vw + 44px + a 55%-wide centered absolute element cannot coexist at 320px — they overpaint; no narrow-width rule adjusts either. Define a ≤480px layout: strip collapses to dot-only + badge, meta overlay hides.

32. **Pause pill's expanded hit-area overlaps the strip's tap target** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — The pause toggle inflates its touch target via `::after { inset:-11px -6px }` (`static/style.css:35917-35921`) while sitting ~10px from the status strip (`static/index.html:1202-1215`), whose tap opens Activity. The invisible pause overlay eats taps aimed at the strip's right edge — pausing all automated work when the user meant "show me what you're doing". Keep the vertical inflation but drop the horizontal (`inset:-11px 0`), or add a gap.

33. **Pause-all confirm is a native `window.confirm`** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `_onPauseToggle` calls `window.confirm('Pause all automated work? …')` (`static/js/applicantActivity.js:115-140`, confirm at `:121`) — the only native confirm left in the applicant chrome (Portal/Remote prefer `styledConfirm`, `applicantPortal.js:77-82`). On mobile the native dialog is jarring, unthemed, and on some Android browsers suppressible ("prevent this page from creating dialogs"), which would make pause **silently impossible**. Use `styledConfirm`.

34. **Zero `safe-area-inset-top` handling under a notch** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — grep: `safe-area-inset-top` = 0 occurrences in `static/style.css`; the app opts into edge-to-edge (`viewport-fit=cover`, `static/index.html:234`) with `apple-mobile-web-app-status-bar-style black-translucent` (`:10`). In the installed PWA the top bar (status strip, pause) and top-anchored toasts (`top:12px` on mobile, `style.css:3605-3608`) render under the iPhone status text/notch. Pad the top bar and `.toast` with `env(safe-area-inset-top)`.

35. **Action toasts land top-right — the dead zone for thumbs — above a bottom-sheet UI** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — Portal uses clickable action toasts (`_toastAction`, `static/js/applicantPortal.js:88-97`) rendered at `top:12px; right:12px` on mobile (`static/style.css:3605-3615`). With all content in a bottom sheet and the user's thumb at the bottom, the tappable "Open" affordance appears at the least reachable corner and auto-hides. On ≤768px, position toasts just above the sheet (bottom-anchored, respecting `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)`).

36. **Status strip truncates to ~13 characters at 320px with no touch fallback** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `max-width:46vw` + `white-space:nowrap` + ellipsis (`static/style.css:32159,32171,32179-32183`): at 320px ≈147px minus dot/padding, "Applying to Acme — filling work history" becomes "Applying to A…". Fine as a teaser — but the full text lives only in `title` (`static/index.html:1202`), invisible on touch; tapping opens the full Activity modal (heavy). A ≤480px tap could first show a one-line expanded toast of the current action.

## Tier 6 — PWA, offline, installability, push plumbing

37. **Manifest still describes the vendored product** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `"description": "Self-hosted AI chat with memory, documents, and tools"` (`static/manifest.json:4`). The Android install sheet / app-info shows a generic chat blurb instead of the job-application agent. Update description (name/short_name are already "Applicant").

38. **`purpose: "any maskable"` on a single unpadded icon crops on Android** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — Both icons declare the combined purpose (`static/manifest.json:12-13`). Maskable icons need ~20% safe-zone padding; serving the same PNG for both means either a cropped maskable render or a shrunken `any` render. Ship separate `-maskable` variants with padding.

39. **`theme_color` hard-coded dark regardless of theme** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `#282c34` in the manifest (`:10`) and the meta (`static/index.html:8`); in light theme the Android status/title bar stays dark over a light app. Add paired `<meta name="theme-color" media="(prefers-color-scheme: …)">` and update it when the workspace theme switches.

40. **Applicant modules are absent from the SW precache — first offline open of the Portal fails** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — The precache lists ~55 shell assets (`static/sw.js:15-63`) but **no** `applicant*.js`, no `emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js`, no `documentLibrary.js`. JS is network-first with cache fallback (`sw.js:116-127`), so these only become offline-available after they've each been loaded once online. A user who installs the PWA, then opens the Portal on the subway, gets module-load failures inside an otherwise-working shell. Add the applicant chain to `PRECACHE` (and keep the comment's "keep in sync" promise, `sw.js:13-14`).

41. **Offline Portal shows locked sections, not an offline state** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — `/api/` is never cached (`static/sw.js:94`), and a failed `/api/applicant/features` refresh greys sections exactly like "never configured" (`static/app.js:1314` branches only on `active`; the `configured`-offline state exists at `workspace/src/applicant_features.py:284-287` — conflation flagged in ux-flows #43). Mobile makes it acute: intermittent connectivity flip-flops the whole nav between unlocked and locked. Cache the last-good features payload (localStorage) and render "offline — showing last known state".

42. **`start_url:"/"` can't launch into the Portal** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `static/manifest.json:5`. The installed app always opens the empty shell; the post-push, post-login home base (Portal) is two more taps (hamburger → rail). Once any URL addressing exists (#4 / ux-flows #1), point `start_url` at the Portal target; until then, auto-open the Portal on launch when pending count > 0.

43. **Per-route Blob manifests risk broken installs from deep routes** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: M] — `static/index.html:163-188` swaps `link[rel=manifest]` to a `Blob:` manifest (SVG-only icons, per-route `start_url`) per route. Chrome's installability criteria are inconsistent with blob manifests + SVG-only icons; a user who hits "Install app" while on such a route may get a degraded or failed install versus the real `/static/manifest.json`. Restrict the swap to non-install contexts or keep PNG icons in the dynamic manifest.

44. **No `navigator.setAppBadge` for pending count** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — The Portal already computes the exact badge number (`static/js/applicantPortal.js:1244-1258`); installed-PWA users on Android/desktop could see it on the home-screen icon via the Badging API — one line next to `_setBadge`, feature-detected. Pairs with #9 (mobile has no visible badge at all today).

## Tier 7 — wizard & forms on a phone

45. **~40-field intake inside an 85vh sheet with a non-sticky footer** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — 12 intake sections (`static/js/applicantOnboarding.js:91-96`), roughly 40 fields (`:1051-1158`), some repeating (work_history `repeat:true`, `:1101`). The per-step Save/Skip footer (`.ao-foot`, `static/style.css:32248-32255`) scrolls with content, so on a phone every step ends with a long scroll hunting for "Save & continue" (and #13 means the keyboard hides it on iOS). Make `.ao-foot`/`.ao-nav` sticky at the sheet bottom on ≤768px with `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` padding.

46. **Wizard help bubbles overflow the viewport at the sheet edges** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `.ao-tip::after` centers itself (`left:50%; translateX(-50%); max-width:280px`, `static/style.css:32269-32278`) with no collision handling; for a "?" near the sheet's left/right edge half the bubble is off-screen at 320px. Clamp within the sheet (flip alignment near edges), and grow the 14px `?` (`applicantOnboarding.js:221`) to a ≥24px touch target.

47. **Vault credential fields block the phone's password manager** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — Google sign-in user/password use `autocomplete="new-password"` (`static/js/applicantVault.js:73-76`). The user is *storing an existing password* — on iOS/Android this suppresses the password-manager fill sheet, forcing manual typing of a strong secret on a phone keyboard (worst-case typo surface for the credential that gates automated sign-ins). 49. **Assistant send shortcut is Cmd/Ctrl+Enter — undiscoverable and largely unavailable on touch** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `static/js/applicantChat.js:199-201`. ## Tier 8 — breakpoint & width edge cases (320 / 768 / 769–834 / landscape)

51. **769–834px tablet portrait: floating windows can overhang the narrowed overlay** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — At ≥769px applicant modals become floating windows sized `min(var(--window-w), 92vw)` (`static/style.css:4702`) inside an overlay narrowed by rail+sidebar (`left: calc(var(--icon-rail-w) + var(--sidebar-w)); width: calc(100% - …)`, `:4590-4600`). At 800–834px with the sidebar open, Remote's `--window-w:980px` (`static/js/applicantRemote.js:60`) resolves to 92vw ≈ 750px+ — wider than the ~500px overlay it must center in, so the window overflows under the sidebar/off-screen. Cap the desktop width against the *overlay* width (`92%` of the overlay, not `92vw`), or extend the sheet breakpoint to ≤834px for the applicant windows.

52. **Landscape phones get a letterboxed 85vh sheet and no adaptation** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — The only max-height media rules tune the sidebar (`static/style.css:1813-1823`); in landscape (viewport ≈ 660×320) the sheet is 85vh ≈ 272px minus grab handle + sticky header — a few rows of Portal or ~3 lines of redline. Add `@media (max-height:480px) and (orientation:landscape)` → sheet `max-height:100dvh`, radius 0, header compacted. (Remote's landscape story is #21.)

53. **Digest panel is a fixed, non-scrolling block above the email grid on mobile** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — The digest injects itself as `flex:0 0 auto` above `#email-lib-grid` (`static/js/emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js:155-159,186-188`), while the mobile email modal makes the *grid* the single scroll owner and hides modal-content overflow (`static/style.css:12336-12341`). A long digest (10 cards × ~90px) therefore consumes the whole sheet height as unscrollable chrome, crushing the email list to near-zero and, at the extreme, clipping its own lower rows. On ≤768px let the digest collapse to a summary bar ("8 matches today — review") that expands within the scrollable grid.

54. **Applications-tab list is a nested 400px scroller inside the sheet** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `.doclib-grid { max-height:400px; overflow-y:auto }` (`static/style.css:11502-11510`) has a mobile override only inside the email modal (`:12336`), not the document library where the Applicant redline lives — producing scroll-within-scroll-within-sheet on a phone (and blocking swipe-dismiss per the ancestor-scroll guard, `static/js/ui.js:1037-1052`). Mirror the email-modal treatment for the doclib on ≤768px.

55. **Digest campaign picker capped at 200px with no touch-visible full name** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `max-width:200px` inline (`static/js/emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js:165-167`); long campaign names truncate in the closed select and no `title` helps on touch. Allow full-width on ≤480px (the header row already wraps, `:161`).

56. **Digest header burns up to 3 wrapped rows of sheet height at 320px** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: M] — Select + four `memory-toolbar-btn`s in one wrap row (`static/js/emailLibrary/applicantDigest.js:161-181`): at 320px this wraps into ~3 rows of chrome before the first job card. Collapse Preview-email / Send-feedback / Quick-survey into a single "⋯" overflow menu on ≤480px, keeping Refresh.

57. **Update surface: 220px log tail at 11.5px monospace during a critical operation** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `.applicant-update-log { max-height:220px; font-size:11.5px }` (`static/style.css:32373-32388`). Watching a self-update from a phone through a 220px window of 11.5px text is squint-grade; on ≤768px let the log fill the sheet (`flex:1`) and bump to 13px.

58. **Mobile has no minimize/dock — every surface switch is a cold teardown** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: M] — `.minimize-btn` and the dock are hidden on ≤768px (`static/style.css:4810-4813`), and closing tears down modal state (ux-flows #10/#37 flagged the desktop registration gap). On a phone this means Portal → redline → back-to-Portal re-fetches and re-renders everything, mid-triage. Cheapest mobile fix: keep the previous sheet's DOM alive-but-hidden for the deep-link chains (Portal↔redline↔remote) so "back" (#4) is instant.

59. **Grab-handle hit zone is ~20px tall on a 4px pill** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — The handle inflates via transparent borders to roughly 4+10+6 = 20px (`static/style.css:4640-4653`) — still under the 44px minimum, and it shares the top strip with the sticky header's tap targets. Since the X is hidden on mobile (#26), the primary dismiss affordance should get a full 44px band (`padding` on the header or a taller `::before`).

60. **`#rail-portal` renders at 0.62 opacity in the mobile-mini rail** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — The Portal launcher is deliberately dimmed until hover (`#rail-portal { opacity:0.62 }`, hover to 1 — `static/style.css:32147-32148`); in the hamburger-summoned mini rail (`:3833-3841`) there is no hover, so the single most important mobile entry point permanently reads as disabled — visually identical to `.applicant-locked { opacity:0.4 }` (`:32135`) one rule above. Set full opacity under `@media (hover:none)`.
