## A. Pre-login: what the front door promises

1. **The landing page never mentions job applications** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `workspace/static/landing.html:338-346` sells "Your own AI workspace… chat, autonomous agents, tools, model serving, email, research". The word "job", "apply", "resume", or "application" appears nowhere in 739 lines. The user's first screen promises a different product than the OOBE delivers ("Welcome to Applicant… connect a model… start applying"). Rewrite the hero + features grid around the actual value proposition: an agent that finds, tailors and files job applications for you 24/7, with review-before-submit.
2. **No "Sign in" / "Open the app" CTA anywhere on the landing page** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — every CTA is `#start` (a git-clone codeblock) or GitHub (`landing.html:347-350,563-566`). A user standing in front of a *running* instance has no path from the marketing page to `/login`. Add a nav + hero "Sign in" button pointing at `/login`.
3. **Landing "Get started" ends at `git clone`** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `landing.html:557-575` tells an already-deployed visitor to clone the repo. For the self-host case the real happy path is `scripts/proxmox-deploy.sh` / `scripts/install.sh`; for a deployed instance it's "log in". The one command shown is the least useful of the three.
4. **Gag testimonials undermine the trust-critical ask** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `landing.html:355-433` ("Loved by enterprises", a screaming cyclops, fake five-star reviews). Charming for a hobby workspace; corrosive for a product that will hold your legal name, EEO data, salary floor and submit things under your identity. Keep the humor, but lead with a serious "what it does / what it never does" section that reuses the wizard's `NEVER_DOES` list (`applicantOnboarding.js:100-105`).
5. **Landing promises features the front-door gates or hides** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the features grid (`landing.html:444-490`) advertises Cookbook, Compare, Email Assistant, Deep Research — vendored-workspace features — and none of the engine's actual differentiators (digest, redline review, live takeover, stealth pre-fill). The pre-login story and the post-OOBE product don't intersect.
6. **Preview panels reference `chat.webm`/`email.mp4` that don't ship** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `landing.html:522,531` point at video sources not present in `workspace/static/`; the panels silently never play. Either ship real captures of the digest → redline → takeover loop (the most convincing pre-login artifact possible) or drop the section.
7. **Landing page hard-codes its own theme, ignoring the app's saved theme** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `landing.html:10-26` re-declares tokens; `login.html:540-558` carefully boots the user's saved theme + bg pattern. Landing → login → shell shifts visual identity twice in the first minute.
8. **No screenshot of the daily payoff pre-login** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — the `shotrow`/`previews` scaffolding exists (`landing.html:235-277`) but shows placeholders. The single highest-converting pre-login asset for this product is a digest morning-view screenshot: "8 matches found overnight, 3 ready for your approval."

## B. Login & first shell load

9. **First-run admin-create mode is undiscoverable from the landing page** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `/login` auto-detects unconfigured auth and flips to "First-time setup — create your admin account" (`login.html:313-319,348-350`) — good — but nothing pre-login tells the operator that visiting `/login` is the next step after `install.sh`. One hero line ("Fresh install? Create your admin account →") closes the loop.
10. **Password policy surfaces only on submit** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — the 8-char minimum is validated after submit (`login.html:405-410`); no inline hint under the password field in setup/signup mode. Losing a first-time admin on a password error is pure friction.
11. **No "what happens next" between account-create and the wizard** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — after setup-mode auto-login the page prefetches and hard-redirects to `/` (`login.html:435-451`); the user lands in an unexplained shell for ~1-2s before the wizard overlay mounts (`static/app.js:1402-1405` awaits a status fetch first). A one-frame "Setting things up…" splash owned by the wizard removes the flash of a locked, foreign workspace.
12. **Engine-down at boot silently skips the wizard AND the Portal** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `maybeLaunchOnboarding()` catches the status-fetch failure and returns `false` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1630-1632`); `app.js:1404-1409` then opens the Portal only if the wizard didn't show — so on engine-unreachable the user gets the bare vendored workspace with every Applicant nav item lock-toasted "Finish setup to unlock this" (`app.js:1360`) and *no signal the engine is down, no retry*. The first-boot race (engine still migrating while the UI is up) reads as a broken product. Detect unreachable distinctly; show "The application engine is starting… retrying" with backoff.
13. **The lock-toast lies when setup is actually complete** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the click-guard toast is always "Finish setup to unlock this" (`app.js:1360`) and the title fallback "unlocks once the Applicant engine is configured" (`app.js:1323`) — both wrong for `present_but_disabled` (dormant build capability) and for engine-down-after-setup. Thread the section's real state into the message.
14. **The "Email / notifications & digests" nav is locked by a step the wizard no longer contains** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — the section requires `channels_configured` (`workspace/src/applicant_features.py:109-117`), but the notifications step was deliberately slimmed OUT of the OOBE into Settings (`applicantOnboarding.js:59-63`). Every fresh user ends the wizard with `rail-email` locked, clicks it, and gets "Finish setup to unlock this" — from a wizard that never mentioned channels and reports itself finished. Either gate that section on `llm_configured` like its peers, or make the toast deep-link to Settings → Notifications.
15. **First shell load renders the full workspace behind the "blocking" wizard** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — the overlay covers a fully-rendered shell (chat, editor, email icons visible but locked). Peripheral UI competes with the wizard; tune the `ow-window` glass backdrop (`applicantOnboarding.js:151-158`) to actually obscure the shell during the first 5 minutes.

## C. The OOBE wizard: structure & navigation

16. **"Skip for now →" is the same button as "Finish" — skip semantics are invisible** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the persistent nav renders one morphing button (`applicantOnboarding.js:275-284`). Skipping "Connect a model" then clicking again "Finish"es into an "Almost there" scold (`_finish`, `applicantOnboarding.js:1576-1600`). Nothing while skipping says *what* skipping costs ("skipping this means Applicant can't start"). Annotate the skip button per-step with the consequence.
17. **Step rail shows "2 of 3" but no time estimate** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `_renderRail` (`applicantOnboarding.js:179-192`) shows ✓/number + title only. The profile step hides a 12-section interview (`INTAKE_SECTIONS`, lines 91-96); the honest label is "Your profile (~10 min, or ~2 min with a resume)". Users quit unbounded forms, not long ones.
18. **The intake's 12 sub-steps are invisible from the rail** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the rail shows 3 top-level steps; inside "Your profile" a second counter appears ("Profile step 3 of 12", `applicantOnboarding.js:1238`). Two disjoint progress systems, and the outer rail never advances during the longest stretch of the funnel. Nest a mini progress bar into the profile tab.
19. **Rail steps aren't clickable — Back is the only way to revisit** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `_renderRail` renders inert spans (`applicantOnboarding.js:182-184`). A user 9 sections deep who wants to fix the model must click Back ~10 times. Completed steps should be jump targets (the `_finish` jump buttons prove the mechanic, lines 1593-1600).
20. **Esc can dismiss the "blocking" wizard mid-step with no confirmation** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the overlay wires `initModalA11y(_overlay, _dismiss)` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1640-1642`) with `_dismiss` as the close callback, so the shared helper's close gestures tear the wizard down mid-upload/mid-form. Unsaved section edits live only in the DOM. Confirm-before-dismiss, or draft-save first.
21. **Mid-section form data is lost on any re-render** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — intake sections persist only on "Save & continue" (`applicantOnboarding.js:1252-1268`); Back, Esc, tab-close, or a failed save discards everything typed (work-history highlights are the painful case). Persist a per-section draft to `localStorage` keyed by campaign+section; rehydrate on render.
22. **Re-entry resumes the right section but shows nothing of what's already banked** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — resume logic reopens at the first incomplete section (`applicantOnboarding.js:1203-1205`) — good — but gives no "you've already saved: resume ✓, identity ✓, 4 roles" recap. State honesty on re-entry rewards coming back.
23. **`_ensureCampaign` silently names the campaign "My job search" with no rename moment** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:123-134`. The user never chooses or sees the name during OOBE, then meets "My job search" in every later surface. One inline editable field on the criteria section fixes it.
24. **Launch predicate mismatch re-opens the wizard forever for profile-skippers** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `maybeLaunchOnboarding` gates on `llm_configured && onboarding_complete` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1634`), but the profile step counts as done at `apply_ready || onboarding_complete` (line 80). A user who supplies essentials via chat/resume but never formally completes the interview gets the wizard re-launched on *every* login, opening on a step the rail shows as done. Align the predicates or persist an explicit "user finished the wizard" bit.
25. **No step-timing telemetry → time-to-first-value is unmeasurable** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — nothing records wizard first-shown, per-step enter/leave, or completion time, so every activation experiment is blind. The engine already receives `POST /setup/advance/<step>` (`applicantOnboarding.js:246`) — timestamp those server-side and add a wizard-shown event for a real funnel report (drop-off per step, median time per section).

## D. Step 1 — Connect a model

26. **A non-admin user is hard-walled at step 1: the endpoint manager's API is admin-only** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — every `/api/model-endpoints*` route the wizard's mounted manager uses is `require_admin` (`workspace/routes/model_routes.py:923,989,1099`), and `getSelectedLlmEndpoint()` swallows the 403 into `null` (`admin.js:2150-2156`). A second (non-admin) user on this multi-user front-door meets the blocking wizard, and Save & continue loops on "Add a model source above, enable it…" (`applicantOnboarding.js:375-377`) forever — the wall is invisible and mislabeled. Either let non-admins ride the admin-configured endpoints (read-only pick), or say honestly "ask your administrator to connect a model" with a detect-admin check.
27. **The wizard silently commits the FIRST endpoint's FIRST model — the user never picks one** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — `getSelectedLlmEndpoint()` returns `data.find(enabled && online && models.length)` and `model: ep.models[0]` (`admin.js:2157-2164`). An Ollama that lists an embedding model or 3B toy first becomes the engine's brain with zero choice UI, and the wizard copy ("make sure it lists at least one model", `applicantOnboarding.js:350`) never says which will be used. Show "Applicant will use: <model> from <endpoint>" with a select before Save & continue.
28. **Local autodetect already exists server-side and the wizard never calls it** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — `GET /api/model-endpoints/probe-local` (`model_routes.py:672`) probes local runtimes, and `_defaultOllamaUrl()` reads `/api/runtime` for the detected Ollama URL (`admin.js:751-760`). §7 #2 asked for autodetect as if it were new work; the sub-item here is pure wiring: on step-1 mount, fire the existing probe and offer "Found Ollama at 127.0.0.1:11434 with 6 models — use it?" as the primary path.
29. **The endpoint manager is mounted raw with zero wizard framing** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `_renderLLM` drops Settings' full power-user manager into the step (`applicantOnboarding.js:347-364`): collapsible Local/API sections, per-model toggles, discover, test — no "recommended path". A first-timer needs a fork: "I have a local model" (one click via #28) / "I have an API key" (one field) / "Show the full manager". Progressive disclosure over the lifted admin panel.
30. **No forced validate-before-continue on the cloud path** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — the manager has a Test button with real result rendering ("Online — found N models", `admin.js:762-777`), but the wizard's Save & continue never requires or suggests it; a bad key surfaces later as the generic "Could not connect this model to the application engine: …" (`applicantOnboarding.js:402-410`). Run a live inference ping before opening the engine gate, with provider-specific failure hints (401 → "check the key"; 404 model → "pick one the endpoint lists").
31. **Step-1 errors collapse four failure modes into two strings** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — (a) no endpoint added, (b) endpoint disabled, (c) endpoint has no models, (d) engine gate rejected — all become "Add a model source above, enable it…" or the gate error (`applicantOnboarding.js:375-377,402-410`). Each has a different fix; name the one that happened ("Your endpoint is added but disabled — flip the switch above").
32. **The gate save always posts `api_key: ''`** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — `applicantOnboarding.js:396-400` assumes the key "already lives with the saved endpoint". If the engine's provider client needs its own key (engine ≠ workspace credential store), the user learns it mid-first-discovery, hours later. Have the engine perform a live completion before reporting `llm_configured`.
33. **`_inferProvider` folds every unknown cloud provider into "openai"** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:426-439`. Groq, Mistral, OpenRouter, Azure-OpenAI (different auth header + api-version) all become `openai`; a wrong guess produces an untraceable downstream failure. Show "Connecting as: OpenAI-compatible" with an override select before saving.
34. **No model-quality guidance at the single most consequential choice** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — the wizard treats a 3B local quant and a frontier model identically, but match scoring, tailoring and cover letters all hang on this pick. One tier hint ("below the recommended tier for resume writing — fine to start, expect rougher drafts") at `_renderLLM` would set honest expectations.
35. **No cost expectation for cloud keys** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — a user pasting an OpenAI/Anthropic key gets no "typically $X–Y/day at 15 applications/day" estimate. Cost shock is a day-2 churn driver unique to autonomous agents; one static sentence at the paste moment is cheap insurance.
36. **The manager relocation can strand Settings if teardown misfires** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `mountEndpointManager` physically moves the Settings node and `_restoreEndpointManager` moves it back best-effort in try/catch (`applicantOnboarding.js:333-345,412`; `admin.js:2115-2142`). A thrown restore leaves Settings → Services with a hole; harden every exit path with `finally`.

## E. The model ladder (escalation tiers) — the invisible second half of "connect a model"

37. **The OOBE never mentions the model ladder — two disconnected model configs** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — the wizard saves a single provider/model to `/setup/llm` (`applicantOnboarding.js:396-400`); Settings separately edits an ordered 1-5 tier escalation ladder at `/setup/llm/tiers` (`applicantModelLadder.js:1-32`). Their relationship (does the OOBE model become Level 1? does saving the ladder supersede the gate model?) is expressed nowhere in UI. One line on step 1 ("this becomes Level 1 — add stronger fallback levels later in Settings") plus a completion-screen pointer makes the architecture legible.
38. **Ladder tiers have no per-tier Test — key typos surface at runtime, mid-application** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `applicantModelLadder.js:172-204` validates only "provider and model present" before PUT; a bad key/URL on Level 3 fails during the first heavy task (resume writing), the worst possible moment. Reuse the endpoint-test pattern (`admin.js:762-777`) per tier row.
39. **Ladder model field is free text with no discovery, next to a manager that has discovery** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `applicantModelLadder.js:109-111` ("e.g. deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash") invites typos the escalation path only hits under load; the endpoint manager 20px away can list models. Offer a datalist fed by the endpoint's model list.
40. **Ladder default tier is OpenRouter cloud — misaligned with the local-first story** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `_blankTier()` defaults to `openrouter` + its URL (`applicantModelLadder.js:41-51`) even when the user's only configured endpoint is local Ollama. Seed the first blank tier from the OOBE-gate model instead.

## F. Step 2 — Your profile (intake, per field)

41. **`repeat: true` sections render only ONE entry — multi-entry work history is impossible to type** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `work_history`, `education`, `references` are declared repeatable (`applicantOnboarding.js:1101,1114,1124`) but `_renderIntakeSection` renders `spec.fields` once with no "Add another role" control, and `_collectForm` flattens inputs into one object (`applicantOnboarding.js:1190-1197`). A career of N jobs becomes one job unless the resume parse filled the rest server-side — kneecapping every downstream Workday pre-fill. Implement repeat-entry cards, or render parsed entries with per-entry edit.
42. **Prefilled-vs-empty fields are visually identical** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — after resume parse the forms render `saved[f.name]` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1236`) with no "from your resume" affordance; the user can't tell machine guesses (need review) from their own input (trusted). A "parsed" chip per prefilled field turns proofreading into a scan.
43. **Identity email/name aren't prefilled from the logged-in account** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — the workspace knows the user; the identity section starts blank (`applicantOnboarding.js:1052-1063`). Prefill `email` and `preferred_name` before the resume even lands — zero-cost derived fields.
44. **`address` is one free-text line feeding structured ATS forms** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: M] — Workday wants street/city/state/zip/country separately; collecting "Mailing address" as one string (`applicantOnboarding.js:1059`) guarantees a lossy split during pre-fill. Structure it now, or parse+confirm the split inline.
45. **Phone is `type: 'text'` with no country-code guidance** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:1058`; `type="tel"` + "+1 555…" placeholder prevents the classic unfillable-Workday-phone-widget failure.
46. **LinkedIn/portfolio URLs aren't validated or normalized** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:1060-1061`; "linkedin.com/in/me" vs full https URL matters to form-fillers. Normalize on save.
47. **Work-authorization country is free text** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `authorized_country` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1067`) gets matched against postings and ATS dropdowns; "USA"/"United States"/"US" divergence creates silent filter misses. A country datalist with ISO names is a 20-line fix.
48. **The sponsorship/visa questions don't branch on the authorized answer** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:1064-1072` asks all four flat; conditional reveal mirrors real ATS branching and prevents contradictory answers the fabrication guard must later reconcile.
49. **Work mode is free text with the enum hidden in the label** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — "Preferred work mode (remote / hybrid / onsite)" as `type: text` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1078`) invites "wfh preferably", unusable for discovery filters. Three checkboxes (multi-select — most people accept 2 of 3) feed the matcher clean enums.
50. **Salary floor: free text, uncoupled currency, no privacy note** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `salary_floor`/`desired_salary`/`currency` are three unlinked text fields (`applicantOnboarding.js:1090-1097`); "$120k" / "120,000" / "120000 USD" land differently in the comparator that gates discovery. Numeric input + currency select + one line on use ("jobs below this are filtered out; never disclosed unless a form requires it").
51. **Target titles as comma-separated text instead of the workspace's tag input** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `titles`/`adjacent_titles` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1085-1088`); the memory/criteria surfaces already render tag chips — lift that input for clean tokenization.
52. **References collected during OOBE — prime deferral candidate** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — references (`applicantOnboarding.js:1122-1131`) are needed by a minority of applications, at submit time, not before first value. Move out of the default path ("Applicant will ask when an application needs one"), shortening the interview by 1/12.
53. **EEO asked at minute ~8 of a product the user hasn't seen work** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:1140-1149`; the defaults are honest ("decline to self-identify") but the ask itself is deferrable to the first application that actually presents an EEO page, where context is self-evident. (§7 #8 covered the inline explanation; this is the sequencing beneath it.)
54. **The EEO control is a 2-option select + free text instead of the canonical enums** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `_fieldHTML` type `eeo` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1178-1186`); real forms have fixed option sets. Free text guarantees a fuzzy-mapping the agent must do later — under a policy of *never guessing* EEO (`NEVER_DOES`, line 103-104). Offer the standard option lists.
55. **The EEO detail input never rehydrates its saved value** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — rendered with `value=""` always (`applicantOnboarding.js:1184`); a returning user sees their choice but not their answer, and can't verify without retyping. Bind `saved[f.name + '__detail']`.
56. **`campaign_criteria` — the highest-leverage input — is section 12 of 12** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — "Describe your ideal next role" (`applicantOnboarding.js:1150-1157`) drives discovery quality and greets the most fatigued user. Move it after `target_roles` and prefill a draft from the parsed resume + titles ("here's what I inferred — edit").
57. **No per-section "why we ask" microcopy** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — only `eeo` and `work_history` carry a `desc` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1100,1142`). One line each ("References: some applications require 2-3; used only with your approval") converts interrogation into collaboration.
58. **Zero client-side required-ness — gaps surface only later as `apply_missing`** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — sections save fully blank (`_collectForm` has no validation); the user learns what actually mattered from the readiness banner afterwards (`applicantOnboarding.js:1213-1226`). Mark the engine's own required-to-apply essentials with the standard required marker in the forms themselves.
59. **Section save failures dead-end generically while holding data hostage in-DOM** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `applicantOnboarding.js:1262-1267`: "Could not save." + re-enabled button; no retry, no draft persistence (see #21), no 4xx-vs-engine-down distinction.

## G. Resume upload & the LaTeX accept/reject gate

60. **Preview failure wedges the resume step — Continue never re-enables** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — after upload the flow runs font-detect then `_buildPreview`, and only on success enables Continue (`applicantOnboarding.js:1420-1423`); on failure only "Preview unavailable: …" renders (`applicantOnboarding.js:1515-1517`) and the sole exit is the global Skip. Since the engine silently degrades when `xelatex`/LibreOffice are missing (CLAUDE.md "Runtime dependencies"), a missing image dep becomes a wedged OOBE. Enable Continue regardless — the preview is an enhancement, not a gate.
61. **The accept/reject decision shows no document — it's a text description of fidelity** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `_buildPreview` renders "(N page(s)). Looks like a faithful match." + notes (`applicantOnboarding.js:1495-1504`) and asks the user to pick "Use this version" vs "Keep my original" *without showing either document*. This choice changes what gets sent under the user's name; render the converted PDF inline (or a first-page thumbnail) side-by-side.
62. **Accept/reject is skippable with an undefined default** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — Continue enables before any choice; the buttons only set a status span (`applicantOnboarding.js:1505-1514`), nothing marks the decision required, and re-entry doesn't reflect a persisted choice. Make it an explicit two-card selection whose state survives re-render.
63. **The inline font gate requires ALL missing fonts before its Continue enables** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — `_renderInlineFontPrompt` unlocks only at `installedCount >= missing.length` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1307-1314`), with no per-font "use a substitute". Most people don't own their resume's font as a `.ttf` file; offer "Continue with substitutes" and state the visual cost.
64. **The font-file ask is expert-level with no help** — [VALUE: med · EFFORT: S] — "Upload font file for **Calibri**" (`applicantOnboarding.js:1286-1293`) assumes the user knows what a .ttf is and where fonts live. Add "where do I find this?" and auto-suggest metric-compatible free fallbacks (Carlito for Calibri, etc.).
65. **The resume is uploaded twice — once to `/base-resume`, again to `/fonts/detect`** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `readResume` re-posts the file it just uploaded (`applicantOnboarding.js:1408-1412`); on slow links this doubles the wait at the funnel's heaviest moment. Run font detection engine-side on the stored document.
66. **The parse-success line reports a raw count with no reviewability** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — "Read 47 details from your resume" (`applicantOnboarding.js:1398`); 47 of what? Link it forward: "identity ✓, 4 roles, 2 degrees, 23 skills — review them in the next steps."
67. **Conflict resolution: one radio pair per conflict, one POST each, no bulk action** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — `_renderConflicts` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1451-1478`) posts sequentially per conflict; ten conflicts = ten choices + ten round-trips. Add "Use resume for all" and batch the confirm.
68. **`.md`/`.txt` resumes get LaTeX-fidelity copy that means nothing** — [VALUE: low · EFFORT: S] — the accept list includes text formats (`applicantOnboarding.js:1352`) but the preview language ("keeps its look") is docx/LaTeX-shaped. Branch the copy and skip font-detect for plaintext.

## H. Completion, first-run defaults, and the dead-air gap

69. **Completion ends at a full stop — "Get started" just closes the overlay** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: M] — `_finish` → `_dismiss` → `refreshApplicantFeatures()` (`applicantOnboarding.js:1567-1569,1603-1620`); no discovery kick, no Portal/Activity handoff (app.js's Portal-open ran at boot, before the wizard, `app.js:1404-1409`), and the user is deposited on the generic chat shell with newly unlocked icons and zero direction. Beat-2 dead air at its exact code site: `_dismiss` should hand off to `openApplicantPortal()` + start a discovery run + say "I'm searching now."
70. **No first-digest ETA anywhere — and the real cadence is "once per UTC day"** — [VALUE: high · EFFORT: S] — the loop delivers the digest at most once per (campaign, UTC day) on whatever tick crosses the boundary (`src/applicant/application/services/scheduler.py:203-207`), not at a user-local morning hour.
